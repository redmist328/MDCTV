MDCTV: A Streamable and Lightweight Neural Vocoder with MDCT spectrum Prediction

Hui-Peng Du, Yang Ai, Fei Liu, and Zhen-Hua Ling

Abstract

Most existing mel-spectrogram-based neural vocoders reconstruct either the raw waveform or the amplitudeâ€“phase spectra from the input mel-spectrogram. The former usually relies on multi-stage upsampling, which leads to high computational complexity, while the latter requires separate modeling of amplitude and phase spectra, placing additional constraints on model design. To address these challenges, this paper proposes MDCTV, the first mel-spectrogram-based neural vocoder that reconstructs the modified discrete cosine transform (MDCT) spectrum. Given a mel-spectrogram, MDCTV first derives an amplitude-spectrum prior with a mel-pseudo-inverse filter, then employs a deep neural network to reconstruct the MDCT spectrum, and finally recovers the waveform via inverse MDCT. To support low-latency applications, we further design a streamable MDCTV by applying both causal convolution and causal normalization. The MDCTV is trained with adversarial learning using a MDCT-domain multi-scale discriminator. Experimental results demonstrate that, compared with advanced mel-spectrogram-based neural vocoders, the proposed MDCTV generates high-quality speech while being lightweight, with substantially reduced model complexity and computational cost.
